Scientific · Deep Learning

SDO Solar Image Pipeline &
Predictive Deep Learning

Undergraduate Thesis · 2025–2026

An end-to-end analytical pipeline that transforms 11 years of raw NASA satellite imagery into actionable space weather insights. I adapted state-of-the-art deep learning architectures (SimCLR & YOLOv5) to process 6-channel 32-bit solar tensors, circumventing the limitations of standard terrestrial models, and applied statistical causality to forecast sunspot formations.

Python PyTorch SimCLR YOLOv5x Granger Causality SunPy & Astropy NumPy Tensors
Physics-Aware Data Pipeline: Processed ~24,000 FITS files into 6-channel float32 NumPy arrays, preserving critical magnetic polarities via max-abs normalization.
Custom Self-Supervised Backbone: Modified ResNet-34 to accept 6-channel inputs and trained it via SimCLR, disabling color-jitter to maintain radiometric integrity.
Transfer Learning Localization: Injected the physics-encoded weights from SimCLR into YOLOv5x to accurately detect active regions without terrestrial pre-training bias.
Spatiotemporal Kinematics: Compensated for the sun's fluid dynamics using the Snodgrass differential rotation model to accurately track feature evolution over time.
Statistical Causality (Forecasting): Conducted Granger Causality tests on 1,262 events, statistically proving that Active Region expansions forecast sunspot formations with an optimal 3-day time lag.
